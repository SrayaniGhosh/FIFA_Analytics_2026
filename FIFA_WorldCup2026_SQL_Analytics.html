Overall Tournament Insights (Part I)

1. Argentina: Most Complete Attack

  • 19 Goals; 15.38 xG; 4324 Passes (Highest).
  • Joint-highest passing accuracy (91%).
  • Messi led with 12 goal contributions.
  • SQL: Joined team/player attacking and distribution tables.

2. Spain: Possession & Control

  • 6 Clean Sheets; only 1 goal conceded.
  • 58% Possession; high forced turnovers.
  • SQL: Integrated team defensive and distribution metrics.

3. France: Attacking Pressure

  • 120 Total Shots; 50 shots on target.
  • 16 Goals; 13.79 xG.
  • Insight: Shot volume correlates directly with attacking success.

4. Efficiency vs. Creation

  • Finishing outliers: Netherlands (+6.00 xG), Japan (+4.83), USA (+4.63).
  • SQL: Calculated (Goals − xG) for efficiency ratings.

Overall Tournament Insights (Part II)

5. Possession & Success

Elite teams (Argentina, Spain, France) consistently led in passing, accuracy, and line-breaking passes, translating ball control into opportunities.

6. Defensive Philosophies

Spain: Prevented chances (1 goal conceded, high pressure). Paraguay: Survived them (Highest saves). SQL distinguishes proactive vs reactive play.

7. Star Player Impact

Messi, Mbappe, Kane, Bellingham, and Haaland dominated across all metrics (xG, Assists, Goals), proving multi-metric evaluation is essential.

8. Discipline Independence

Aggression (fouls/cards) did not negatively correlate with attacking success. High-pressing stars like Messi and Bellingham remained top performers.

Key Takeaway: Relational database analysis enables a nuanced understanding of tactical trade-offs, defensive efficiency, and individual star influence.

How SQL Enabled Football Performance Analytics

SQL Capabilities Demonstrated

  • Database normalization into multiple relational tables.
  • Foreign-key joins linking players, teams, and performance statistics.
  • Aggregate functions (COUNT, SUM, AVG) for tournament summaries.
  • Ranking using ORDER BY and LIMIT.

Derived Metrics & Analytics

  • Goal Contributions: (Goals + Assists).
  • Efficiency: (Goals − xG).
  • Multi-table analytical joins across attacking, defending, distribution, discipline, and goalkeeping datasets.
  • Comparative ranking of players and teams across multiple performance dimensions.

Project Conclusion

This project demonstrates how SQL transforms raw football statistics into meaningful performance insights through relational database design and analytical querying. By integrating multiple normalized tables, we successfully evaluated performance across attacking, defensive, and individual dimensions.
